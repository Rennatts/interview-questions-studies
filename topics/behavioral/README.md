# Behavioral — STAR stories & interview prep

Behavioral interviews evaluate how you work: communication, ownership, collaboration, decision-making, and how you handle ambiguity. The goal is to have a small set of well-prepared stories you can adapt to many questions.

## How to use this file

- Build a **story inventory** (6–10 stories) and reuse them across prompts.
- Answer using **STAR** (Situation, Task, Action, Result) + a short **Reflection** (what you learned).
- Keep stories concrete: what *you* did, trade-offs, constraints, and measurable outcomes.

---

## Core framework

### 1) What is STAR?

**Short answer**:
- **S**ituation: context (team, product, constraints)
- **T**ask: your responsibility / goal
- **A**ction: what you did (decisions, communication, implementation)
- **R**esult: impact (metrics, outcomes, lessons)

**Add-on**: **Reflection**
- What you’d do differently next time, and what you learned.

---

### 2) What makes a strong behavioral answer?

**Short answer**:
- Specific scope and constraints (time, stakeholders, system limitations)
- Clear ownership (“I did X”, not “we did X”)
- Trade-offs and reasoning
- Measurable outcomes (latency, conversion, incidents reduced, dev velocity)
- Collaboration details (how you influenced, aligned, resolved disagreement)

---

### 3) Common pitfalls to avoid

**Short answer**:
- Vague stories with no decisions
- Over-indexing on tech details without the human/process part
- Missing results (“and then we shipped”)
- Blaming others
- Long setups (keep Situation/Task brief; spend time on Action/Result)

## Story inventory (what to prepare)

### 1) Recommended story set (6–10)

**Short answer**: Prepare at least one story for each theme:
- **Ownership**: you drove a project end-to-end
- **Ambiguity**: unclear requirements → you clarified and shipped
- **Conflict**: disagreement → you aligned stakeholders
- **Failure**: incident/bug → you fixed and prevented recurrence
- **Leadership**: mentored, improved process, raised quality bar
- **Performance**: improved LCP/INP, reduced bundle size, fixed jank
- **Accessibility**: improved a11y, fixed keyboard/screen reader issues
- **Collaboration**: cross-team coordination (backend/design/product)
- **Delivery**: shipped under deadline with trade-offs
- **Learning**: picked up a new domain/stack quickly

## Common behavioral questions (with what they’re really testing)

### 1) “Tell me about yourself”

**Testing**: clarity, role fit, narrative.

**Good structure**:
- Present: what you do now
- Past: 1–2 key experiences
- Future: what you want next + why this role

---

### 2) “Tell me about a time you disagreed with a teammate”

**Testing**: conflict resolution, empathy, decision-making.

**What to include**:
- The disagreement and stakes
- How you listened/asked questions
- How you aligned on a decision (data, experiments, principles)
- Outcome and relationship impact

---

### 3) “Tell me about a time you made a mistake”

**Testing**: accountability, learning, prevention.

**What to include**:
- What happened and impact
- How you fixed it
- Guardrails you added (tests, monitoring, rollout)

---

### 4) “Tell me about a time you led without authority”

**Testing**: influence, alignment, communication.

**What to include**:
- How you created clarity (doc/design proposal)
- How you got buy-in (stakeholders, trade-offs)
- How you unblocked execution

---

### 5) “Tell me about a time you dealt with ambiguity”

**Testing**: product thinking, requirements discovery.

**What to include**:
- How you asked questions and defined success criteria
- How you de-risked (spike/prototype, phased rollout)

---

### 6) “Tell me about a time you improved performance/reliability”

**Testing**: engineering judgment, measurement.

**What to include**:
- Baseline metric (LCP/INP, error rate, p95 latency)
- Root cause analysis
- Changes shipped
- Before/after numbers

---

### 7) “Tell me about a time you had to push back”

**Testing**: prioritization, assertiveness, stakeholder management.

**What to include**:
- What you said no to and why (risk, scope, quality)
- Alternative proposal (phased approach, MVP)
- Outcome

## Practice drills

### 1) 60-second version vs 3-minute version

**Short answer**: Practice each story at two lengths:
- **60s**: the gist (high-level)
- **3min**: full STAR + reflection

---

### 2) Follow-up question rehearsal

**Short answer**: For each story, practice answering:
- What trade-offs did you consider?
- What would you do differently?
- How did you measure success?
- What was the hardest part?

## Negotiation prep (compensation, level, scope)

### 1) What’s the goal of negotiation (high level)?

**Short answer**: Align on a package and scope that reflect your level and market value while keeping the conversation collaborative. The goal is not “winning”; it’s reaching a sustainable agreement.

---

### 2) What information should you gather before negotiating?

**Short answer**:
- Role/level expectations (scope, leadership, impact)
- Compensation components (base, bonus, equity, signing, refreshers)
- Location/remote policy and cost-of-living adjustments
- Timeline (when you need a decision; competing offers)

---

### 3) What are good negotiation principles to mention in interviews?

**Short answer**:
- Don’t negotiate until you have a clear signal of interest (offer stage).
- Negotiate the full package, not just base.
- Use data points (market ranges, scope) and be specific about trade-offs.

---

## Company-specific prep (principles mapping)

### 1) How do you prepare for company-specific leadership principles?

**Short answer**:
- Identify the company’s principles (or values) and map your stories to them.
- Prepare 1–2 stories per high-priority principle, plus one “failure/learning” story.
- Use the same story with different framing depending on the principle being evaluated.

**Example mapping approach**:
- Principle: “Ownership” → story where you drove a project end-to-end and handled trade-offs.
- Principle: “Customer obsession” → story where user feedback changed your plan.
- Principle: “Bias for action” → story where you shipped an MVP with guardrails.

---

### 2) What makes a principle-to-story mapping convincing?

**Short answer**:
- Explicitly state the principle in your answer (“This is an ownership example because…”).
- Show the decision-making and trade-offs, not just the outcome.
- Include measurable results and a reflection.

---

## Salary/leveling negotiation (practical checklist)

### 1) How do you talk about leveling without sounding defensive?

**Short answer**:
- Anchor on scope and expectations (“What does success look like at level X?”).
- Share evidence: examples of impact, leadership, ambiguity handling, and system-level decisions.
- Ask for calibration: “Which level matches the scope you want me to operate at?”

---

### 2) What are the main levers in negotiation?

**Short answer**:
- Level (scope + band)
- Base salary
- Bonus
- Equity (grant + refreshers)
- Signing bonus
- Start date / flexibility

## Project deep-dive script (your “one big project”)

### 1) What is a project deep dive?

**Short answer**: A structured walkthrough of one project where you can demonstrate ownership, technical depth, trade-offs, and results. Many interviews become a deep dive even if they start as “tell me about a project”.

---

### 2) A strong deep-dive outline (5–8 minutes)

**Short answer**:
- **Problem**: user pain + business goal
- **Constraints**: timeline, tech, team, scale
- **Architecture**: components, data flow, API integration
- **Key decisions**: 2–3 trade-offs you made and why
- **Execution**: how you shipped safely (tests, rollout, monitoring)
- **Results**: metrics + qualitative impact
- **Lessons**: what you’d do differently

---

### 3) Common follow-ups to prepare for

**Short answer**:
- “Why did you choose X over Y?”
- “What was the hardest bug/incident?”
- “How did you measure success?”
- “What would you change if you had 2 more weeks?”

---

## Suggested practice exercises

- Write 8 story cards using the template above.
- Record yourself answering 3 questions and evaluate:
  - clarity, pacing, STAR completeness, measurable results
- Pick one story and adapt it to 3 different prompts (conflict, leadership, ambiguity).
- Write a 1-page deep-dive doc for your best project and practice delivering it in 6 minutes.
- Write a negotiation checklist for your next offer (package components, ranges, questions).
- Pick one company and write a principles-to-stories mapping (at least 6 principles → 1 story each).
- Write a “leveling narrative”: 5 bullets that demonstrate scope at your target level.
